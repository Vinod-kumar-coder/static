Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic monuments in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is located in the city of Agra, on the banks of the Yamuna River in the state of Uttar Pradesh. The monument was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal and was completed in 1653. The Taj Mahal is famous for its breathtaking white marble architecture, intricate carvings, beautiful gardens, and symmetrical design. It is often regarded as a symbol of eternal love and is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The monument changes its appearance throughout the day, reflecting different shades under sunlight and moonlight, which makes it even more attractive to visitors. Tourists can reach Agra through various modes of transportation. The city is well connected by road, rail, and air. Visitors can travel by train from major cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur. Agra also has an airport with domestic flight connections, and many tourists prefer traveling by car or bus via modern expressways. The Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors every year because of its historical significance, architectural beauty, and romantic legacy. It stands as a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and remains one of the most visited tourist destinations in the world.
